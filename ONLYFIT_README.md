# Project OnlyFit (codename)

> **Software-first calisthenics training system.**  
> *Fitness shouldn’t be random.*

## What we are building (first 12 months)

OnlyFit is a **progression-based calisthenics training system** that helps beginners build **foundation strength** (pull-up, dip, core, scapular strength) with a clear, structured path.

**Out of scope (first 12 months):**
- Selling supplements/equipment/clothing
- Open creator marketplace for anyone
- “Chat with coach” as the main value prop

Those can come later **only after** the core system proves retention and paid conversion.

---

## What makes sense in your roadmap (and what doesn’t)

### ✅ What’s solid
- **Workout Creator first**: great for validating UX and your own daily use.
- **Foundation programs + progression tree next**: directly solves the beginner problem (“I don’t know what to do / if I’m progressing”).
- **Creator marketplace later**: scalable content supply once your “quality bar” and structure are proven.
- **Coaching as a premium layer**: can monetize strongly, *if* it’s constrained to the system.

### ⚠️ What’s risky / illogical (as described)
1. **Marketplace → then add coaching chat**
   - Risk: you become a messaging platform with operational burden.
   - Fix: keep coaching “structured” (plans + check-ins + progress review) before “always-on chat”.

2. **Dietitian system via subscription chat**
   - Risk: regulated/high-stakes guidance, high ops, hard to quality-control.
   - Fix: start with **nutrition templates + logging + educational content**. Add paid dietitian access only when you can enforce quality and boundaries.

3. **Commerce (equipment/supps/clothing) too early**
   - Risk: shifts perception to e-commerce, kills trust, adds logistics, refunds, support, compliance.
   - Fix: do **contextual recommendations** first (non-transactional), then affiliate, then marketplace much later.

## Recommended order (clean, scalable, low-risk)

### Phase 1 — Workout Creator (Builder-first)
**Goal:** make creation/tracking frictionless; prove you will use it daily.

**Scope**
- Create/edit workouts (exercises, sets, reps/time, rest, notes)
- Workout library (user’s own)
- Simple history (completed workouts)
- Basic analytics: volume, frequency (minimal)

**Non-goals**
- No built-in programs
- No marketplace
- No coaching features

**Success metric**
- You use it 4–5 days/week for 3–4 weeks without friction.

---

### Phase 2 — Foundation Programs + Progression Tree (Core value)
**Goal:** beginners stop quitting because they finally know what to do next.

**Scope**
- Your curated **foundation strength programs**
- Progression tree for key pillars (pull, push, core, scapula)
- “Gated progression”: unlock next step by **performance + minimum time**
- Simple explanations: *what this step builds and why it matters*
- Beginner onboarding (level check / baseline test)

**Non-goals**
- No open creator uploads
- No direct messaging

**Success metrics**
- Week-2 retention improves
- Users complete 6–10 sessions in first month
- First paid conversion possible (optional)

---

### Phase 3 — Invite-only Creator Marketplace (Content scaling)
**Goal:** scale content without destroying quality.

**Scope**
- Invite-only coaches/creators publish programs **inside your structure**
- Review / approval flow
- Revenue share / program sales (commission)
- Clear standards: progression rules, evidence-based constraints

**Non-goals**
- Not open to everyone
- No spammy “PDF program dump”

**Success metrics**
- Marketplace programs have completion rates comparable to yours
- Low refund/dispute rate

---

### Phase 4 — Coaching Layer (Premium service, structured)
**Goal:** higher ARPU without becoming “WhatsApp in-app”.

**Start with (recommended)**
- Coach assigns plans from the system
- Weekly check-in form (sleep, soreness, compliance)
- Progress review notes + adjustments
- Optional limited messaging (async) with boundaries

**Avoid early**
- Unlimited chat as the product
- Real-time calls inside app (ops heavy)

**Success metrics**
- High renewal rate
- Coach quality remains consistent (ratings, outcomes)

---

### Phase 5 — Nutrition (low-risk → higher-touch)
**Step 1 (safe)**
- Macro/calorie targets, meal templates
- Educational modules
- Habit tracking

**Step 2 (later)**
- Dietitian marketplace (invite-only)
- Boundaries + disclaimers + clear non-medical scope where required

---

### Phase 6 — Commerce (very late, only if it fits)
**Step 1**
- Contextual recommendations (bands, rings, bar, etc.)
- Non-transactional links / affiliate

**Step 2**
- Curated store (drop-ship/partners), minimal SKUs

**Step 3**
- Full marketplace (if and only if you have scale)

---

## What to add (high leverage)
- **Onboarding baseline test** (simple, not perfection): how many push-ups, dead hang time, etc.
- **Clear “why” explanations** in Phase 2: 1–2 sentences per step.
- **Progress visibility**: “You’re here → next milestone → why it unlocks”
- **Quality gate rules** for creators (Phase 3): templates, required fields, progression constraints.

## What to remove or postpone
- Unlimited messaging as early core feature
- Dietitian “chat subscription” as early product
- Selling supplements/equipment/clothing before you have a strong core + retention

---

## Monetization (when it becomes reasonable)
- Phase 2: optional premium (advanced tracking / extra programs)
- Phase 3: commission on program sales
- Phase 4: coaching subscription / revenue share
- Phase 5: nutrition premium + dietitian access (invite-only)
- Phase 6: affiliate → curated commerce

---

## Product principle (non-negotiable)
> **Users can customize, but the system can’t be random.**  
Progression must remain structured, explainable, and measurable.

## Glossary
- **Progression Tree:** a structured map of steps from foundation to mastery.
- **Gated Progression:** next step unlocks when readiness is proven (metrics + time).
- **Foundation Strength:** pull-up/dip/core/scapular capacity to support skills later.
